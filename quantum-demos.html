Bloch sphere — orthogonal projection
Measuring in the Z-basis projects the state vector onto the Z-axis. The squared projection length gives the Born probability. φ encodes relative phase and invisible to Z-basis measurement.

Projective (von Neumann) measurement
A projective measurement is a rotatable orthonormal basis {|m₀⟩, |m₁⟩}. The outcome probability equals the squared projection of |ψ⟩ onto that axis. In projective measurement, the two axes are always perpendicular.
State angle α60°
Basis angle β — rotate measurement0°
Outcome probabilities
P(m₀) = cos²(α − β) 0.750
P(m₁) = sin²(α − β) 0.250
Post-measurement collapse → |m₀⟩

Dashed lines drop perpendiculars from |ψ⟩ to each axis. Rotating β to align with |ψ⟩ gives P(m₀) → 1.

POVM — trine measurement
A POVM replaces orthogonal projectors with positive operators {Eₖ} satisfying only ΣEₖ = I. The trine has 3 non-orthogonal outcome directions, thereby producing more outcomes than dimensions.
